\section{Experimental setup}
\Sm ~Coulomb excitation experiment.

Beam: \Sm (4.65 MeV/$u$, 651 MeV)

Target: \Pb


ISOLDE experimental hall:
\begin{align*}
	\text{p}^+ \rightarrow~ &\text{Ta} \rightarrow \text{Produces the chart of nuclides up to Ta} \rightarrow \text{GPS} \rightarrow \\
	&\text{RILIS} \rightarrow \text{REX-EBIS} \rightarrow \text{HIE ISOLDE} \rightarrow \text{MINIBALL}
\end{align*}

GPS: Selects mass 140

RILIS: Selects Sm with laser

REX-EBIS: Excites nucleus in three steps ionizing the atom $\rightarrow$ nucleus in high charge state

HIE-ISOLDE: Linear accelerator

Small angle: Forward scattering: Larger distance, weaker \textcolor{red}{EM}-field, less excitation probability.

Large angle: Backward scattering: Closer distance, stronger \textcolor{red}{EM}-field, higher excitation probability.
